About this coin

What better way to celebrate 25 years of being two Wrubels than with a silver 2 ruble coin?

This coin was minted in 2001, as part of a limited series featuring notable Russian figures. This coin is #225 of the pressing, though we don't know how many total were minted.

Vladimir Ivanovich Dal (1801-1872)

The coin honors Vladimir Dal, one of Russia's most remarkable cultural figures. A lexicographer, writer, and physician, Dal devoted 53 years of his life to creating the monumental "Explanatory Dictionary of the Living Great Russian Language" (Толковый словарь живого великорусского языка).

His dictionary contained over 200,000 words and captured the richness and diversity of the Russian language as it was actually spoken by people across the vast empire. It remains one of the most comprehensive and beloved dictionaries of Russian, still referenced by scholars and language lovers today.

The obverse of the coin depicts Dal with his famous dictionary, accompanied by small figures representing the Russian people whose living language he so faithfully documented.

The Coin Inscriptions

Obverse (Dal side):

  • "Толковый словарь живаго великорускаго языка" — "Explanatory Dictionary of the Living Great Russian Language"
  • "В. Даль" — Dal's signature
  • 1801-1872 — His lifespan

Reverse (Eagle side):

  • "Два рубля" — "Two Rubles"
  • "Банк России" — "Bank of Russia"
  • 2001г. — Year of minting (our wedding year!)
